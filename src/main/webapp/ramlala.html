RAMLALA

RAMLALLA IDOL

The idol is made from 'shyamal' (full dark) stone and crafted by artist Arun Yogiraj of Mysuru. The Ram Lalla idol portrays the deity as a five-year-old child standing on a lotus also crafted from the same stone. The idol weighs 200 kg, and boasts a height of 4.24 feet and a width of 3 feet.

Ram Lalla(child rama) is a term used to refer to the infant Lord Rama, a central figure in Hinduism and the protagonist of the Hindu epic, the Ramayana. The term “Lalla” means “child” or “infant,” and “Ram” refers to Lord Rama. The phrase “Ram Lalla” is often used affectionately to depict the divine form of Lord Rama as an infant.

The much-awaited 'pran pratishtha' ceremony of the revered idol of Ram Lalla casts a spotlight on the gifted sculptor behind the creation, Arun Yogiraj. Deeming himself "the luckiest person on earth", Arun, 41, represents a fifth-generation legacy of sculptors from Agrahara, Mysuru, Karnataka.26 Jan 2024
